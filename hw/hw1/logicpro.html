The beginings of Logic Pro started in the mid to late 1980's. Gerhard Lengeling and Chris Adam developed a MIDI sequencer which was a pattern based sequencer. During the time most MIDI sequencers presented a song as a linear set of tracks. Eventually track-based sequencing overtook pattern-based sequencing. The C-lab programmers left that company to form Emagic which in 1993 released Notator Logic. Notator Logic attempted to put together track-based and pattern-based operation. Apple aquired Emagic in 2002 and the rest is history. Logic was developed in C, C++, Objective-C, and Swift. They used these languages for speed within the program. Swift was used because logic is only available on Apple products.
